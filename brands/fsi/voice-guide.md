## Brand position

The Five Star Institute is the authority in mortgage industry education and
professional development. FSI speaks as a trusted industry insider — authoritative,
substantive, and collegial. It treats its audience as experts, not students.

## Voice pillars

**Authoritative** — FSI leads the field. Write with confidence. Avoid hedging.

**Professional** — B2B audience of executives and working professionals.
Formal enough to match the room; not stiff or bureaucratic.

**Collegial** — FSI is part of the industry it serves. Use "we" and shared
language. Acknowledge shared challenges.

**Direct** — Mortgage professionals are busy. Lead with value.

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Conference / event copy | Energetic, prestigious — highlight who attends, what's at stake |
| Course / certification | Clear, outcome-focused — lead with results, not curriculum |
| News / editorial | Neutral, informative — facts first |
| Email marketing | Direct, short paragraphs, clear CTA |
| LinkedIn | Professional, first-person insight |

## Word choice

Use: professionals, practitioners, leaders, servicers, originators,
education, certification, professional development, outcomes, results.

Avoid: "cutting-edge," "game-changing," "guru," excessive exclamation points,
passive voice as default, consumer-brand register.

## Length guidelines

| Format | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Blog post | 600–1,200 words |
| Event description | 150–300 words |
| Course description | 100–200 words |
| Meta description | 140–160 characters |
| Email subject | 40–60 characters |
